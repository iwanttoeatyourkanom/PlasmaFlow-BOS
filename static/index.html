These values (peak, mean, coverage) are qualitative diagnostic indicators in arbitrary grayscale units (0–255), not physical measurements. They are based on intensity difference, which mixes real refractive-index change with lighting, camera movement, and noise. Use them to compare runs under the same setup, not as absolute gas quantities. A reference-vs-reference control shot is needed to establish the noise floor before these numbers can be trusted.

Capture Guide

How to shoot reliable reference / test pairs

Camera setup

  • Lock everything to manual: focus, ISO, exposure, and white balance. Auto settings can change between the two shots and look like a fake signal.
  • Use a tripod. Use the self-timer or a remote so you never touch the camera between the reference and test shots.
  • Take the reference (flow off) and test (flow on) back-to-back. Do not move the camera, nozzle, or background in between.

Background & lighting

  • Fill the frame with the speckle background. Keep it flat and wrinkle-free.
  • Light the background evenly and brightly. Avoid glare or reflections going straight into the lens.
  • For plasma runs, bright background lighting helps wash out the faint purple glow so it doesn't count as signal.

Distances (keep fixed and write them down)

  • Keep the nozzle-to-background distance around 10 cm so results match the earlier gas runs.
  • Record camera, nozzle, and background distances every time in the parameter fields.

Get reliable numbers

  • Take a control pair first: two "flow off" shots. Run them through the tool to see your noise floor before judging any real signal.
  • Draw an ROI around the jet so the numbers come from where the flow is, not the whole image.

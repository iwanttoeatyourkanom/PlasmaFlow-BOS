User Guide

How to shoot, and how each feature works
Guide

This tool shows gas flow you cannot normally see. It lines up two photos of a speckled background, one with the flow off and one with it on, and maps where the background shifted. Brighter areas mean a bigger shift.

Five steps

  1. Upload a Reference (flow off) and a Test (flow on).
  2. Optional: drag a box on the preview to set an ROI so numbers come from the jet, not the whole frame.
  3. Optional: set a Scale so sizes read in mm before measuring.
  4. Press Analyze.
  5. Read the images and numbers, then Download ZIP to keep the results and CSV log.

First thing to do

  • Before trusting any result, shoot two flow-off photos and run them as a Noise Baseline. That shows how much signal is just noise on your setup.

Camera

  • Lock focus, ISO, exposure, and white balance to manual. Auto modes drift between shots and fake a signal.
  • Use a tripod. Trigger with a timer or remote so you never touch the camera between shots.
  • Shoot Reference and Test back to back. Do not move the camera, nozzle, or background in between.

Background and light

  • Fill the frame with the speckle pattern. Keep it flat and wrinkle free.
  • Light it evenly and bright. Avoid glare straight into the lens.
  • For plasma runs, bright light helps wash out the faint glow so it does not read as signal.

Distances

  • Keep the nozzle to background gap fixed, around 10 cm, so runs stay comparable.
  • A bigger gap gives a stronger signal for weak flow, but keep it the same within a set of runs.
  • Write camera, nozzle, and background distances in the parameter fields every time.

For mm sizes

  • Put a ruler or a mark of known length flat on the background, in the same plane as the flow.
  • Keep it in the Reference photo so you can trace it in the Scale card.

Modes

  • Single Analysis: one Reference against one Test. Use this for a quick check, a one-off shot, or once you already know the actuator works and just want today's numbers.
  • Plasma On/Off Compare: one Reference against two Tests (plasma off and on), same ROI and settings, shown side by side with OFF vs ON vs ratio for every metric. Use this when you need to prove the shift is from the plasma itself, not from camera drift, background noise, or heat off the nozzle — the OFF column is your control.
  • Not sure which to pick: if the question is "does the actuator do anything," use Compare. If that's already answered and you're just logging a run, Single is faster.

ROI

  • Drag a box on the preview. Peak, mean, and coverage then come from inside the box only.
  • Skip it to use the whole frame. Lock an aspect ratio first if you want a fixed shape.

Compare Regions (signal vs background)

  • Every box you drag is added to a list. Mark one as Background (no flow) and the rest as Signal (the jet).
  • After Analyze you get a table with SNR, the signal mean divided by the background mean. Near 1 means the signal is buried in noise.

Scale (mm)

  • Trace a known length in the Reference photo and type its size in mm.
  • Line length and jet width then read in mm everywhere. The scale is remembered until you clear it.
  • Re-set it if you change zoom or camera distance.

Noise Baseline

  • Add a second flow-off photo (Reference 2). The tool diffs Reference against it, both off, to show pure noise.
  • If your real result looks like this baseline, it is probably noise, not flow.

Display settings change how the image looks only. Measurement settings change the numbers saved to the CSV.

Display only

  • Colormap: color scheme for the pseudo-color image.
  • Gain: brightens faint flow so it is easier to see.
  • Noise floor: hides pixels below a level so weak background noise goes black.

Changes the numbers

  • Coverage threshold: a pixel counts as flow when its shift is above this. Higher is stricter.
  • Align: corrects tiny camera or background shifts, measured from the corners only, before the diff.
  • Denoise: a light blur on the diff to cut per-pixel sensor noise before measuring.

Auto buttons

  • Auto locks a slider and fills a sensible value when you press Analyze. It reads noise from a Background-tagged Compare Region if you've added one, otherwise the whole image -- add a Background region for a tighter, more accurate suggestion. Click Auto again to set it yourself.

Reading the numbers

  • Peak and Mean are brightness shifts from 0 to 255, not physical units. Use them to compare runs on the same setup.
  • Coverage is the share of pixels above the threshold.
  • SNR near 1 means signal and noise look the same. Well above 1 is a real signal.
  • A weak or missing plasma signal is a normal, real result. Do not force it.

Extra views

  • Show ROI Box draws your boxes over the images.
  • Raw is the untouched diff, no align or denoise. Thresholded is the black and white coverage mask.
  • Zoomed Signal ROI crops to the jet at full resolution.

Export

  • Download ZIP saves the images plus two CSVs: the run log and the region table.
  • The CSVs share one run ID so they join back together. mm columns are filled when a scale is set.
